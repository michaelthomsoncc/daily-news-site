Funding Freeze Hits Hard

The United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs reports a brutal shortfall. Only 21 percent of the $45.3 billion needed for life-saving aid in 2025 has been funded so far—just $9.6 billion in the pot. That's a sharp drop from previous years, leaving programs on the brink. In places like Sudan and Gaza, where conflicts rage, families can't get basics like clean water or medicine.
