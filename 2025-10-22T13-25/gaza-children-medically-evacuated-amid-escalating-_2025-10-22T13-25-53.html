Health System on the Brink, Kids in Critical Need

The World Health Organization (WHO) is sounding the alarm: Gaza's health system is teetering on total collapse. Hospitals are overwhelmed, short on meds, power, and staff, making even basic care impossible. UN audio briefings from October paint a grim picture, with thousands trapped in the crossfire. Specifically, over 12,000 people, including at least 5,000 children, are in critical condition and desperate for evacuation. "More than 12,000 critically ill and injured patients, including at least 5,000 children, urgently need to be evacuated from Gaza," warns WHO's top official in the region. These aren't just numbers—they're kids who can't wait for aid trucks stuck at borders.

UN Steps Up Medevac Missions Amid Intensifying Clashes

With fighting ramping up, UN-facilitated medical evacuations are the only way out for the most vulnerable. Recent operations have airlifted Gaza children from the war zone to safer spots for treatment, but it's a drop in the bucket. The UN's October updates note that "Gaza children among latest medical evacuations from war zone," pulling young patients from rubble-strewn areas where ambulances can't even reach. Back in the UK, this means aid donations and awareness campaigns are picking up steam—think how your pocket money could fund a medevac flight. But delays in scaling up these rescues leave many kids' futures hanging by a thread, as borders stay sealed and supplies dwindle.
