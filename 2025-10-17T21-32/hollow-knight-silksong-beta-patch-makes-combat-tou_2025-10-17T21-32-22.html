Hollow Knight: Silksong Beta Patch Makes Combat Tougher

From the Game Drops and Patches section – Straight facts, no filter.

Imagine slicing through Pharloom's shadows as Hornet, only to find every boss fight hitting harder. Team Cherry just dropped a beta patch for Hollow Knight: Silksong, tweaking the game's difficulty without dialing back the challenges. Released back in September 2025 for Switch, PS5, Xbox, and PC, this update—Patch 4 beta—keeps the Metroidvania grind intense, especially in combat. If you're grinding levels after school in the UK, expect no mercy from those silk-thread dodges.

Patch 4 Beta Hits the Scene

Team Cherry rolled out the Patch 4 beta on October 16, 2025, hot on the heels of Patch 3. This isn't a full release yet—it's for testers to try before it goes wide. The big change? A major tweak to controller inputs, making responsiveness sharper for joystick warriors. No more laggy swings in tight platforming spots. As one update notes, "Team Cherry certainly are getting these patches out rapidly." For UK gamers on Nintendo Switch, this means smoother sessions during rainy afternoons, but only if your setup syncs right.

Combat Gets No Break

The patch notes scream challenge: "If you were hoping for a further decrease in Hollow Knight: Silksong's difficulty, it's not coming here." Combat feels tougher, with enemy patterns amped up and no easy modes added. Hornet's needle strikes demand pixel-perfect timing now, as beta tweaks refine hitboxes without softening blows. Players report bosses like the silkweavers landing heavier combos, forcing smarter silk use and quicker reflexes. In global playtests, this has sparked debates—some love the raw edge, others grind through retries. For a 12-year-old tackling it solo, it's a test of patience, mirroring the daily school hustle of pushing limits.

Global Buzz and UK Grind

Worldwide, the beta's live on Steam and other platforms, pulling in testers from Australia to Europe. GameSpot's October 16 article highlights how these changes keep Silksong's core tough, post its September 4 launch across consoles. In the UK, where Hollow Knight fans filled forums last year, this patch lands amid half-term breaks—perfect for diving deep, but brace for frustration if you're new to Pharloom's quests. X posts from October 16 echo the vibe: "Hollow Knight: Silksong's New Beta Patch Notes Don't Make Things Any Easier." No nerfs to hard challenges; instead, subtle buffs to enemy AI make every encounter stick.

Controller and Bug Fixes

Beyond combat, the beta squashes bugs from earlier patches, like sticky doors in city houses fixed back in 2021 updates for the original game—echoing Team Cherry's polish obsession. Controller remapping gets a revamp, aiding UK players on PS5 or Xbox who swap between schoolwork and silk-slinging. It's all about flow: no more input delays disrupting your rhythm in interconnected kingdoms.

Watch for the full Patch 4 rollout soon—Team Cherry's rapid fixes suggest more tweaks ahead. If combat's your jam, this beta proves Silksong stays unforgiving, building skills that carry over to real-life grinds. Dive in, but save often.
