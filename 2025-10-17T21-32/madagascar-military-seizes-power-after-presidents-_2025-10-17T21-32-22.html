The Takeover Unfolds

Colonel Michael Randrianirina, head of an elite unit called CAPSAT, stepped up outside the presidential palace on October 15. He announced the military was forming a new government, ending Rajoelina's rule amid chaos from youth-led street protests. These weren't just rallies—thousands hit the streets demanding change after years of economic gripes and political tension. Randrianirina, a low-profile army figure until now, led the rebellion that ousted the president. Reports confirm Rajoelina bolted on a French military flight from Sainte Marie Airport, heading to safety as soldiers moved in.

Randrianirina Steps into the Spotlight

Who is this guy? Before last weekend, most in Madagascar hadn't heard of Colonel Michael Randrianirina. But on October 17, he was sworn in as president amid cheers, trumpets, and raised swords. Reuters witnessed the ceremony, where the coup leader promised stability after the power grab. The military's move followed a national gendarmerie unit seizing control on October 13, right in front of government officials. It's all tied to those protests that started boiling over weeks ago, with demands for Rajoelina's resignation echoing loud.
