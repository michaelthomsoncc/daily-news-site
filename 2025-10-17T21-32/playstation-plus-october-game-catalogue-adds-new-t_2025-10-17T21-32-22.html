PlayStation Plus October Game Catalogue Adds New Titles

Sony just dropped the October 2025 PlayStation Plus Game Catalogue, packing in fresh titles for Extra and Premium subscribers. Announced on October 15, this lineup hits right as UK gamers gear up for half-term breaks, blending horror remakes and action epics that could dominate your weekend queues. With global buzz spiking on socials like X—where posts from users like Mazen Turkmani hailed the reveal under #PlayStationPlus—these adds promise to shake up daily play sessions across borders.

Headliners: Silent Hill 2 and Until Dawn Lead the Charge

The big draws are Silent Hill 2, the chilling remake from Bloober Team, landing October 22 for Extra and Premium tiers. This survival horror classic, rebuilt with modern graphics, dives into foggy nightmares and psychological twists—perfect for UK players dodging rainy afternoons. Then there's Until Dawn, the interactive thriller from Ballad Entertainment, also arriving October 22. Choose-your-own-adventure style, it puts you in control of teen survivors on a snowy mountain, where one wrong call means game over. These recent releases, fresh from 2024 launches, mean PS Plus now rivals buying them outright, saving parents' wallets amid rising game prices.

Action and Adventure Boost: V Rising and Yakuza Expand the Mix

V Rising joins on October 29, Stunlock Studios' vampire survival game where you build castles and hunt in an open world. For UK gamers grinding after school, it's a deep dive into crafting and boss fights that could stretch playtime for weeks. Yakuza: Like a Dragon drops October 22 too, Ryu Ga Gotoku Studio's turn-based RPG shifting the series to Ichiban Kasuga's underdog story in Yokohama. Packed with side quests and mini-games, it mirrors real-world city vibes, hitting home for London or Manchester players spotting familiar urban chaos. These titles, announced amid global stock shortages, ensure PS5 owners worldwide get value without FOMO.

Indie Gems and Classics Round It Out

Smaller but punchy adds include Poppy Playtime: Chapter 1 on October 22, Mob Entertainment's creepy toy factory horror—short but scary, ideal for quick scares before bed. As Dusk Falls arrives same day, Interior/Night's narrative drama blending live-action choices across generations, echoing UK soap opera drama in pixel form. Wizard with a Gun caps it October 29, Galvanic Games' roguelike shooter mixing magic and bullets in a wild west fantasy. For Premium users, Tekken 3 enters the Classics Catalogue October 22, the 1998 fighter with arcade-perfect ports and rollback netcode, fueling online rivalries across Europe. All verified from Sony's October 15 blog post, these land as PS Plus hits 40 million subs globally, per recent reports.
