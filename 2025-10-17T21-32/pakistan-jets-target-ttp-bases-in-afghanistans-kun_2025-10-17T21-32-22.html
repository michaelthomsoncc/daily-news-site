Pakistan Jets Target TTP Bases in Afghanistan's Kunar Province

From the Global Conflict Updates section – Straight facts, no filter.

Imagine your favorite battle royale game, but with real jets screaming over mountains, hitting enemy hideouts. On October 16, 2025, Pakistani fighter jets blasted bases of the Tehreek-e-Taliban Pakistan (TTP), a Taliban splinter group, in Afghanistan's rugged Kunar Province. This strike ramps up the border fireworks between the two neighbors, turning a tense standoff into full-on aerial action amid a conflict that's been heating up for weeks.

The Strike Details

Pakistani jets targeted TTP strongholds in Kunar, a province hugging the Pakistan border known as a hotspot for these militants. Posts on X from Apexguard reported the hits straight from the afternoon of October 16, calling it a direct assault on TTP bases. Kunar districts like Marawara, Sarkano, and Dangam—prime TTP turf—saw multiple explosions, echoing earlier drone strikes that wounded kids and sparked protests. No official Pakistani claim, but the jets used were likely JF-17s, the same type that hit hard in past ops, killing dozens including civilians in cross-border raids.

Escalation in the Bigger Fight

This isn't a solo match; it's part of the 2025 Afghanistan-Pakistan clash. Pakistan's been pounding TTP spots since early October, including a big airstrike in Kabul on October 9 aiming for TTP leader Noor Wali Mehsud. That one hit a market, killing 15 civilians, per reports. Afghanistan's Taliban fired back with border attacks on October 11-12, hitting Pakistani posts. A 48-hour ceasefire kicked in on October 15, but these Kunar strikes show it's shaky. Taliban summoned Pakistan's ambassador after similar blasts, protesting the incursions into their turf.

Impacts on the Ground

Locals in Kunar felt the boom—houses wrecked, like one in nearby Nangarhar hit twice, leaving two kids missing and others hurt. TTP affiliates, including the FAH group, lost seven leaders in a related Kabul precision strike, according to X updates. Pakistan says it's hunting terrorists who launch attacks from Afghan soil, with over 30 dead in a single Khyber Pakhtunkhwa airstrike last month. For everyday folks, it's chaos: markets bombed, borders locked, and families caught in the crossfire.
