Government Faces 1.5 Million Homes Miss

The Chartered Institute of Housing (CIH) warns the government will likely miss its 1.5 million homes target by 25%, based on fresh research. That means around 375,000 fewer homes than promised, hitting young families hard in cities where space is gold for setting up PC rigs or console zones. Labour's emergency measures kick in now, focusing on speeding up new builds to close the gap. "We're on track for stretching but achievable delivery," ministers insist, despite the Office for Budget Responsibility (OBR) flagging a potential 200,000 shortfall.
