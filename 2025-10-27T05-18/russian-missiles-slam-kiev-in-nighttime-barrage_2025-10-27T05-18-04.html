Strike Breakdown: Missiles and Drones Hit Hard

Russian forces unleashed a nighttime assault using missiles and drones, zeroing in on Kiev's thermal power plants and key substations. According to reports, the attacks aimed to cripple energy supplies, with explosions lighting up the sky across the capital. Ukrainian air defenses intercepted some threats, but others broke through, causing widespread blackouts in residential areas. "Kiev thermal power plants and substations were again targeted," noted local updates, echoing a pattern of infrastructure hits that have left the city on edge. This follows Ukraine's recent drone strikes on Russian oil depots, as the tit-for-tat escalates from the frontlines to urban centers.

Casualties and City Chaos

The barrage claimed two lives and injured 13 in Kiev alone, with officials confirming strikes on energy sites rippled across Ukraine. Debris scattered through districts, sparking fires and forcing evacuations in the dead of night. Power grids buckled, plunging neighborhoods into darkness and halting daily routines—think no lights for homework or charging controllers after school. Ukrainian authorities tallied damage to critical infrastructure, warning of prolonged outages as winter looms. "Russian missiles and drones hit sites in Ukraine overnight, including infrastructure and energy sites," stated officials, underscoring the human toll amid the machinery of war.
