Tardigrades: Nature's Ultimate Tank

Tardigrades, aka water bears, are microscopic beasts that thrive in extremes—think boiling heat, freezing cold, or deadly radiation blasts. A key study spotlights a protein they crank out called Dsup, which shields their DNA from X-ray damage. "A protein produced by tardigrades... protects and repairs DNA damaged by X-rays," researchers note. These critters curl up in a tun state, drying out to survive space-like voids, then revive like pros. From Genes to Space Shields

Scientists at the University of Tokyo tested Dsup by inserting the gene into human cells. Results? Those cells resisted radiation way better, cutting DNA breaks by nearly half under X-ray fire.
