Sony Files Injunction Against Light of Motiram Developers

From the Game Dev Buzz section – Straight facts, no filter.

Imagine grabbing your controller for a new open-world adventure, only to hear Sony slamming the brakes with a court order. That's the drama unfolding right now: Sony's pushing hard for a legal block on Light of Motiram, an upcoming game from Chinese giant Tencent. They say it's ripping off their blockbuster Horizon series, sparking a massive industry clash that's got gamers worldwide buzzing. Filed just yesterday, October 17, 2025, this injunction could halt promotions and shake up release plans—perfect timing as UK kids like you gear up for holiday gaming sessions.

The Fresh Court Move

Sony dropped their big push in a US court on October 17, asking for a preliminary injunction against Tencent. This isn't just talk; it's a direct bid to stop Light of Motiram from hitting pre-release hype. The game, an adventure title blending exploration and robot battles, was announced earlier this year. Sony's lawyers argue it's too close to their Horizon worlds, where Aloy fights mechanical beasts in lush, post-apocalyptic lands. "Damage is done," Sony blasts in filings, slamming Tencent's defense as "nonsense." This escalates a lawsuit kicked off back in July 2025, when Sony first called Light of Motiram a "slavish clone."

What's at Stake in the Lawsuit

At its core, this is about copyright infringement. Sony claims Tencent copied key elements like character designs, world-building, and gameplay mechanics straight from Horizon Zero Dawn and Horizon Forbidden West. Court docs detail how Light of Motiram's protagonist and creature fights mirror Aloy's bow-slinging action. Tencent fired back with a motion to dismiss, but Sony's new filing opposes it head-on, urging the judge to block any promo pushes. No trial date yet, but this injunction could freeze things fast—meaning no trailers or betas for now. For UK gamers, who snapped up millions of Horizon copies on PS5, it hits home: your fave series' legacy is on the line.

Tencent's Pushback and Industry Ripples

Tencent, the world's biggest gaming company by revenue, isn't backing down. Their motion to toss the case argues Sony's claims don't hold up legally, but Sony counters that waiting would let the "knock-off" flood the market. Light of Motiram was set for a global rollout, potentially on PC and consoles, drawing hype for its stunning visuals and story. This feud echoes bigger tensions: Western devs versus Chinese powerhouses, with billions in stakes. In the UK, where PlayStation dominates school chats and after-school grinds, a win for Sony could protect jobs at Guerrilla Games (the Horizon studio in Amsterdam, but with UK ties via Sony Europe). Lose, and copycats might dilute what makes games unique.

Global Gaming Shake-Up

Breaking news hit X (formerly Twitter) via gaming insider MithrieTV on October 17, highlighting the injunction as a "major blow" to Tencent's plans. Posts lit up with UK fans debating if Light of Motiram truly copies or innovates. No UK-specific court angle yet, but Sony's London office is tracking it closely—impacts could delay imports or spark price hikes if legal fees ripple out. Daily grind? Your next Horizon DLC might feel safer, but watch for Tencent retaliating with their own hits like Genshin Impact sequels.

Keep eyes peeled: court hearings could drop rulings by late 2025, deciding if Light of Motiram launches or gets shelved.
