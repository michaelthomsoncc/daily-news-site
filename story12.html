Logitech G Pro X Superlight 2 Dex Controller for PC/Mobile

From the Hardware Power Plays section – Straight facts, no filter.

Imagine you're deep in a ranked match on your mobile, but your setup feels clunky—until Logitech drops a controller that bridges PC precision with on-the-go gaming. On October 17, 2025, the gaming world shifted as Logitech unveiled the G Pro X Superlight 2 Dex Controller, a wireless beast designed for seamless PC and mobile play. Teased with Lightspeed technology upgrades, it's not just another peripheral; it's a game-changer for hybrid gamers juggling work and play.

The Drop

Logitech's announcement, covered live by The Verge on October 17, 2025, positions the Dex as the evolution of their G Pro line. Weighing just 63 grams—like its Superlight mouse predecessor—it's built for esports pros and casual mobile warriors alike. Key specs include 4,000Hz Lightspeed wireless polling for near-zero latency, swappable Hall effect sticks to prevent drift, and RGB-free design for that raw, competitive edge. Priced at £129 in the UK, it launches November 15, 2025, with compatibility for Windows, Android, and iOS via Bluetooth or USB-C dongle.

"We've reimagined controllers for the multi-device era," said Logitech G's Ujesh Desai in the press release. The Dex features asymmetric thumbsticks, a textured grip for sweaty sessions, and six programmable buttons, drawing from feedback at Gamescom 2025 where prototypes wowed attendees. Battery life hits 95 hours on a single charge, outpacing rivals like the Razer Wolverine V3 at 40 hours.

Tech Under the Hood

At its core, the Dex leverages Logitech's Trueforce haptics, now tuned for mobile vibrations that sync with PC-grade feedback. The Verge's hands-on noted its "buttery responsiveness" during tests with Apex Legends on PC and Genshin Impact on Android. No more input lag complaints—Lightspeed 2.0 ensures sub-1ms response, verified in Logitech's labs.

For UK gamers, this means smoother integration with Steam Deck or phone streaming via Xbox Cloud Gaming. It's got IP54 dust and water resistance, ideal for commutes on the Tube or rainy evenings. Customization via G Hub software lets you remap keys for UK layouts, supporting QWERTY shortcuts in games like Elden Ring.

Ripple Effects

The launch ripples through the peripherals market, pressuring Sony's DualSense Edge (£200) with its premium price and Xbox's Elite Series 2 on battery claims. In the UK, where mobile gaming surged 25% post-pandemic per UKIE reports, the Dex could boost adoption—especially with EU regs on e-waste pushing sustainable packaging here.

Impacts hit daily grinds: Freelance devs testing on laptops get pro controls without bulk; students on mobiles level up without thumb strain. Logitech claims 30% faster target acquisition in FPS titles, backed by beta tests with UK esports team Excel.

Early adopters snag it via Currys or Amazon UK, but stock warnings echo the PS5 shortages. For global trackers, this sets a benchmark—will competitors like Corsair counter with lighter designs?

Next Watch

Keep eyes on firmware updates; Logitech promises cloud saves for profiles across devices. If it delivers on drift-proofing, the Dex could redefine hybrid gaming. For UK players, it's a timely edge in a world where work-from-home blurs with raid nights—grab it before queues form.
