Fresh Packs Launch with UK Curriculum Tie-In

On October 30, 2025, Minecraft surged to the top of global most-played lists with 140 million monthly active users, smashing Fortnite's 120 million. The secret sauce? New education packs designed to slot right into the UK history curriculum. These aren't random worlds; they're built around key events like the Industrial Revolution and World War II home front, letting kids recreate factories or air raid shelters block by block. Following earlier snapshots from late October that added UK-inspired villages and Redstone gadgets mimicking British inventions like the steam engine, this update makes history interactive. Schools across England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland can now use these for Key Stage 3 lessons, blending gaming with GCSE prep without ditching the fun.

Global Reach Meets UK Roots
