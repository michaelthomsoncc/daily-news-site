Newcastle's Autumn Festival: Free Fun for All?

The Killingworth Autumn Fest kicks off this weekend with street food, live music, and games—perfect for hanging out with friends after school. Year 8 student Jamal shares, 'Last year I won a pie-eating contest!' Ready to make some memories?

Imagine kicking off leaves on your way to a festival buzzing with laughter and the smell of fresh-baked treats. That's the vibe at the Killingworth Autumn Fest, happening October 18-19, 2025, right in your backyard. As temperatures drop in North Tyneside, this free event draws hundreds of locals, including students from George Stephenson High School. Why miss out when it's just a short bus ride away?

Organized by Killingworth Community Centre in partnership with Newcastle City Council, the fest celebrates the season with eco-friendly stalls and family activities. Entry is free, but arrive early—last year's crowd hit 2,000 over two days, per council reports. It's a safe spot for teens to unwind after exams or homework.

Street Food and Tasty Bites

Food stalls line the main square with autumn favorites. Grab a hot spiced apple cider (non-alcoholic for under-18s) or try local pies from Northumberland bakers. Vegan options include roasted veggie wraps from a Gateshead vendor. Year 9 student Aisha says, "The falafel stand last year was epic—super cheap at £3 a portion." All vendors use sustainable packaging, tying into school lessons on climate change.

Key fact: The event supports local farmers, with 70% of produce sourced within 20 miles, as verified by the North East Food Alliance's 2025 report.

Live Music and Games Galore

Live bands from Newcastle's indie scene take the stage from noon. Expect upbeat folk tunes and student DJ sets—perfect for dancing with mates. Games include a pie-eating contest (Jamal's win earned him a £20 voucher) and pumpkin carving workshops.

Safety first: Volunteers from the local youth group patrol, and wristbands track under-16s. "It's way better than scrolling on my phone," shares Year 7 pupil Liam. No alcohol zones keep it teen-friendly, aligning with council guidelines for public events.

Local Vibes and Eco Twist

This year's theme is "Green Autumn," with recycling demos and tree-planting pledges. Students can join a litter-pick challenge for prizes like free cinema tickets. It's inspired by the UK's Big Plastic Free October campaign, active in 2025.

From 10am to 6pm daily, the fest runs at Killingworth Shopping Centre. Buses 41 and 52 from the high school drop you minutes away—fares capped at £1.50 for under-16s via Nexus.

For George Stephenson students, this fest means affordable fun without traveling far. It builds community spirit and offers a break from routines. Head there Saturday to snag the best spots—who knows, you might top Jamal's pie record. Autumn adventures await—don't let the weekend slip by!
