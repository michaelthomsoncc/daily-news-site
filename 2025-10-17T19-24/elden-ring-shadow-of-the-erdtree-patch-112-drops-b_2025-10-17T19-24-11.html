Elden Ring Shadow of the Erdtree Patch 1.12 Drops Balance Tweaks

From the Epic Game Updates section – Straight facts, no filter.

FromSoftware has unleashed patch 1.12 for Elden Ring, zeroing in on Shadow of the Erdtree with a slew of balance tweaks that promise to shake up the Lands Between. Dropped on October 17, 2024, this update hits consoles and PC simultaneously, addressing player feedback on overpowered builds and underperformers since the DLC's June launch. For UK gamers grinding through late-night sessions, it's a timely recalibration—available now via auto-update on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and Steam, clocking in at around 300MB to keep your Tarnished sharp without bloating storage.

The Drop

Official patch notes from FromSoftware detail targeted adjustments to weapons, sorceries, and armor, all grounded in community data from months of Shadow of the Erdtree play. Key buffs include increased attack power for the Black Steel Great Hammer, Bloodfiend's Arm, and Greatsword—each seeing a 7-10% boost to physical damage, per the notes. Nerfs hit aggressive skills like Seppuku, reducing its bleed buildup by 20% on repeated uses, and Blind Spot's FP cost jumps from 5 to 8, curbing spam in PvP duels.

Sorceries get love too: Stars of Ruin's homing projectiles now track better, with a 15% range extension, while Ranni's Dark Moon sees FP efficiency improved by 10%. Heavy armor stability bonuses rise across the board—up to +2 for sets like the Bull-Goat—making tank builds more viable against DLC bosses like the Divine Beast Dancing Lion. The update also adds official PS5 Pro support for 60 FPS at 4K, a boon for UK players on upgraded rigs chasing smoother frame rates amid the UK's variable broadband scene.

Ripple Effects

These changes land hard on daily grinds, easing progression for magic users frustrated by post-DLC scaling—IGN reports early tests show sorcery builds clearing Bayle the Dread 15% faster. Weapon tweaks balance the meta, nerfing bleed-heavy setups that dominated Colosseum fights, as quoted in FromSoftware's notes: "Adjustments have been made to prevent certain weapons from becoming excessively powerful." For UK Tarnished, it's practical: no server downtime means seamless integration into your evening queue, but expect a PvP shakeup—Reddit threads already buzz with respec rushes at Rennala.

Incantations like the Fingerprint Stone Shield now block 25% more physical damage, per official deets, bolstering faith tanks against Scadutree boss rushes. No story alterations, just pure mechanical fine-tuning to keep the DLC's challenge fair without gutting its brutality.

Community Pulse

IGN's coverage highlights the patch's focus on "long-term balance," noting over 50 individual tweaks drawn from telemetry since patch 1.10. Player impacts are immediate: builds relying on the old Seppuku cheese see viability drop, forcing hybrid experimentation. In the UK gaming scene, where Elden Ring still tops Steam charts per HLTV data, this reinforces FromSoftware's iterative support—vital for commuters battling London traffic with handheld play on Steam Deck, now optimized with better input latency.

Quotes from Bandai Namco's PR echo the intent: "These updates refine the experience based on global feedback," ensuring the DLC's 20 million+ sales base stays engaged.

Patch 1.12 solidifies Shadow of the Erdtree's legacy as a balanced expansion, but watch FromSoftware's next moves—rumors of further PvP modes linger in official forums. For UK grinders, dive in; your next boss run just got a real edge.
