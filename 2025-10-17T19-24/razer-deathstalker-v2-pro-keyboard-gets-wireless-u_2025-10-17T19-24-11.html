Razer DeathStalker V2 Pro Keyboard Gets Wireless Upgrade

From the Gear Grind section – Straight facts, no filter.

Razer has unveiled an upgraded version of its DeathStalker V2 Pro keyboard at Computex 2025, introducing wireless capabilities with an 8000Hz polling rate. This refresh targets gamers seeking low-latency performance without cables, building on the original's optical switches. Tom's Hardware's hands-on review from the event highlights the device's seamless Bluetooth and 2.4GHz connectivity, clocking in at under 1ms latency in tests.

The Drop

The new DeathStalker V2 Pro ditches the wired-only design of its 2022 predecessor for full wireless freedom. Razer's optical-mechanical switches remain, offering 1mm actuation and 70-million keystroke durability. Key specs include a 4000mAh battery lasting up to 200 hours, per Razer's claims. Tom's Hardware noted, "The 8000Hz polling rate via the updated HyperPolling dongle makes it one of the fastest wireless boards we've tested, rivaling wired competitors like the Corsair K70 Max."

UK availability starts November 2025 at £229.99, positioning it against Logitech's G915 Lightspeed (£219) and SteelSeries Apex Pro Wireless (£279). The low-profile chiclet keys suit extended sessions, with RGB lighting synced through Synapse 4 software.

Tech Under the Hood

At Computex, Razer demoed the keyboard's analog optical tech, allowing variable actuation for gaming precision—think sniper shots at 0.1mm trigger. The review praised ABS keycaps for their matte finish and PBT stabilizers reducing wobble. Wireless upgrades include adaptive frequency hopping to minimize interference in crowded 2.4GHz spaces, crucial for UK homes with multiple devices.

Build quality holds up: aluminum top plate, 930g weight for stability. No hot-swappable switches, but Razer's focus is on plug-and-play reliability. Tom's Hardware measured input lag at 0.4ms wired-equivalent wirelessly, a boon for competitive titles like Valorant or Call of Duty.

Ripple Effects for UK Gamers

For daily grinds, this means untethered setups in compact UK flats—pair it with a Razer mouse for a full wireless ecosystem. Energy efficiency shines; the battery sips power during idle RGB modes. Impacts include better esports viability, as UK teams like Excel Esports eye low-latency gear for tournaments.

Drawbacks? No wrist rest included, and the £230 price stings amid inflation. Compared to 2024's wired DeathStalker V2 Pro at £200, the wireless premium adds value for mobile pros but not casuals. Global supply chains, post-Computex, promise stock by Q4 2025, dodging earlier chip shortages.

What's Next

Watch for firmware updates enabling 8000Hz on existing V2 Pros via dongle sales. Razer's Computex tease of modular add-ons could expand this line. For UK trackers, it's a solid upgrade if you're wired-bound—test in-store at Currys or Scan for that optical snap.
