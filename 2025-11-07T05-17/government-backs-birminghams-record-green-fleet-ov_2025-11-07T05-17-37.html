The Big Switch: How Birmingham's Going Green

Birmingham's council just sealed the deal on replacing its entire bus fleet with electric ones, backed by UK government grants totaling millions. This overhaul, kicking off years ago with the Clean Air Zone in 2021, now hits full throttle. Over 100 new electric buses rolled out in the last week alone, powered by sustainable tech that slashes carbon emissions by up to 90%. "This leads the charge on net zero," blasts the local council in their fresh statement. It's all about hitting the UK's 2050 net-zero goal, with Birmingham leading municipal moves like a boss-level upgrade in an open-world game.
