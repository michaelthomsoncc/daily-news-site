Trump's Tariff Twist

President Trump stirred the pot on October 17, threatening steep tariffs on Chinese goods, including tech components. But he quickly dialed it back, telling reporters the high levels are "not sustainable" and assuring everyone it "will all be fine" with China. Futures markets dipped overnight, with S&P down 0.07% and Nasdaq off 0.31%, before recovering on his comments. This flip-flop shows how fragile the US-China tech truce is, especially for AI hardware that powers next-gen gaming engines.

AI Chips Under Fire

Tariffs on AI chips from China could jack up costs for everything from high-end GPUs to mobile gaming tech. Reports from October 17 highlight how export curbs are already biting, slowing innovation in AI-driven features like procedural worlds or voice assistants in games. For UK imports, this means higher prices on kit from brands reliant on Chinese manufacturing—your next PS6 equivalent or PC upgrade might cost 10-20% more, squeezing family budgets amid daily grinds like school and saves.

British markets, hooked on global tech flows, face direct hits. With the pound under pressure from these tensions, importing gaming gear from Asia gets tougher.
