Corsair Launches K100 RGB Air Wireless Keyboard

From the Hardware Power Plays section – Straight facts, no filter.

Imagine chaining headshots in Valorant without a whisper of input lag, even untethered from your rig. For UK gamers glued to global tech drops, Corsair's just unleashed the K100 RGB Air Wireless Keyboard—a beast promising sub-millisecond responsiveness in a cord-free package. Debuting today, October 17, 2025, it's engineered for esports pros and daily grinders alike, cutting through the wireless noise with polling rates that rival wired setups.

The Drop: Specs Straight from the Source

Corsair's K100 Air lands as their lightest full-size wireless keyboard yet at 850g, packing Cherry MX Ultra Low Profile mechanical switches for that crisp, optical feel. The standout? An 8,000Hz polling rate via Slipstream wireless tech, delivering 0.125ms latency—faster than many wired rivals, per PCWorld's hands-on review today. "It's like the keyboard reads your mind before you press," notes reviewer Andrew E. Freedman, who clocked it syncing flawlessly with the new iCUE software for real-time polling tweaks.

Battery life hits 100 hours on 2.4GHz or Bluetooth, with per-key RGB lighting that's fully customizable. Hall-effect tech on the media controls and a magnetic wrist rest round it out, all in a slim 22mm profile. Priced at £249.99, it's available now via Corsair's UK site and retailers like Scan and Overclockers.

Performance Under Fire

In PCWorld's October 17 tests, the K100 Air aced wireless benchmarks, outpacing Logitech's G915 in polling consistency during marathon sessions. Freedman reported zero dropouts over 10 meters, even through walls—ideal for UK setups in compact flats where desk space is premium. The low-profile keys demand adaptation, but once dialed in, they slash fatigue for long raids in World of Warcraft or coding marathons.

Impacts hit hard on the daily grind: For competitive players tracking CS2 metas, this means edge in reaction times without cable clutter. UK gamers facing import delays from US launches will appreciate the immediate stock— no more FBA roulette on Amazon.
