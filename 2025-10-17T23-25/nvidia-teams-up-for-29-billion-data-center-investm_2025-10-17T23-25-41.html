Nvidia Teams Up for $2.9 Billion Data Center Investment

From the Hardware and Tech Boosts section – Straight facts, no filter.

Imagine powering up your favourite UK battle royale or crafting epic worlds in Minecraft, but on a global scale. Nvidia, the graphics giant behind the GPUs that make your games look stunning, just dropped a massive $2.9 billion deal with Australia's Firmus Technologies. This investment is building huge data centers down under, supercharged by renewable energy to handle AI and computing like never before. For gamers like you, it's a step towards smoother online play worldwide, as these centers boost the infrastructure that keeps cloud gaming and multiplayer sessions running fast.

The Big Partnership Unveiled

On October 16, 2025, Nvidia announced its team-up with Firmus Technologies, an Australian startup. The goal? Create a fleet of AI data centers powered entirely by renewables. According to reports, this $2.9 billion push will roll out across Australia, starting with two sites already underway in Melbourne and Tasmania. These spots pack 150 MW of power and use Nvidia's cutting-edge GB300 chips, set to go live by April 2026. "Nvidia Corp. is partnering with Australian startup Firmus Technologies Ltd. to create a massive fleet of renewable energy-powered artificial intelligence data centers across the country," stated Bloomberg.

Scaling Up for the Future

Dubbed Project Southgate, the initiative aims to hit 1.6 gigawatts by 2028. That's enough juice to run thousands of high-end gaming rigs non-stop. Firmus and Nvidia are focusing on green energy sources, tying into Australia's push for sustainable tech. Posts on X from October 16 highlighted the excitement: "Nvidia partners with Australian startup Firmus on a $2.9B AI data center project featuring 150 MW power." This adds to Nvidia's worldwide network, which already supports everything from game rendering to AI training that could one day enhance your in-game buddies with smarter NPCs.
