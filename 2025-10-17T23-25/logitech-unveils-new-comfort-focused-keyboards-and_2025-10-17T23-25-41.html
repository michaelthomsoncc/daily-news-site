Logitech Unveils New Comfort-Focused Keyboards and Mice for 2025

From the Hardware and Tech Boosts section – Straight facts, no filter.

Picture this: you're deep into a late-night Fortnite session, fingers flying, but your wrists ache after hours of grinding. Logitech just dropped news that's set to change that for gamers worldwide, including us in the UK. On October 16, 2025, they unveiled a fresh lineup of peripherals laser-focused on comfort and speed, launching throughout the month. This isn't hype—it's gear built to keep you in the game longer without the pain.

New TKL Keyboard: Compact Power for Quick Plays

At the heart of the launch is the new tenkeyless (TKL) keyboard from Logitech G. Ditching the number pad makes it ultra-portable, perfect for UK gamers squeezing sessions between school and homework on smaller desks. It's designed with enhanced comfort features, like improved key spacing and responsive switches that cut down on fatigue during marathon matches. As per the announcement, this keyboard prioritizes "speed and precision" for competitive edges in games like Call of Duty or Rocket League. Launching soon in October, it'll hit shelves with options for wireless connectivity, meaning no more cable tangles messing up your setup.

Wireless Headset: Immersive Audio Without the Wires

Logitech didn't stop at typing—they added a wireless gaming headset to the mix. This bad boy promises crystal-clear sound for pinpointing enemy footsteps in Apex Legends, all while keeping your head comfy with lightweight build and plush ear cups. The focus on comfort shines through in its extended battery life, letting you game for hours without recharging mid-raid. Global rollout starts this October, and for UK players, it means easier access via retailers like Amazon or Currys, syncing seamlessly with PS5 or PC for that daily grind boost. No more pausing to adjust cords; it's freedom to move.

Ultra-Light Mouse: Speed Meets Endurance

Topping it off is an ultra-light mouse that's a game-changer for fast-paced action. Weighing next to nothing, it glides effortlessly, reducing hand strain during intense mouse flicks in Valorant or Overwatch. Logitech emphasized "enhanced speed" in the reveal, with a high-precision sensor that tracks every micro-movement accurately. Comfort details include an ergonomic shape fitting smaller hands—ideal for 12-year-olds leveling up their skills. Available throughout October 2025, this mouse will land in the UK market, potentially slashing those wrist cramps that hit after school-time sessions.

These updates from Logitech land right when global gaming scenes are exploding, with esports events pulling in UK crowds.
