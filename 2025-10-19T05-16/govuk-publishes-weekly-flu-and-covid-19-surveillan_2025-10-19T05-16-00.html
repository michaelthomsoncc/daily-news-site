Flu Cases Climbing in Schools and Homes

Week 41 data from GOV.UK flags flu activity ramping up, especially in primary care spots. GP consultations for flu-like illnesses hit higher numbers than the week before, with rates at 10.5 per 100,000 people in England. That's up from previous lows, signaling the start of seasonal spread. COVID-19 Holding Steady but Watchful

COVID-19 positivity in pillar 2 tests stayed low at around 5.2% for week 41, but hospital admissions crept to 14 per 100,000 in some regions. GOV.UK notes ongoing surveillance via UKHSA labs, confirming variants like JN.1 circulating but no big surges. In England, wastewater sampling shows stable viral loads, not spiking yet. Vaccination Push to Level Up Defenses

England's autumn campaign kicked off strong, with over 1.2 million flu jabs given by week 41's end, per GOV.UK figures. Outbreaks on the Radar
