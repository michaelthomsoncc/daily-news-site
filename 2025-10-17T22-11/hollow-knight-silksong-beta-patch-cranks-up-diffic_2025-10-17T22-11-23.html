Hollow Knight: Silksong Beta Patch Cranks Up Difficulty

From the Game Patch Drops & Reveals section – Straight facts, no filter.

Imagine leaping through Pharloom's shadowy ruins in Hollow Knight: Silksong, only to face bosses that hit harder and platforms that demand pixel-perfect jumps. Team Cherry just dropped a beta patch for Patch 4 on October 16, 2025, and it's not dialing back the challenge—it's keeping the heat on, making every silk-thread swing count even more.

The Patch That Stays Tough

Released just yesterday, the beta for Silksong's Patch 4 tweaks gameplay without softening the edges. GameSpot reports that if you're waiting for an easier ride through Hallownest's sequel, this update won't deliver. Instead, it focuses on fixes like a major controller input overhaul, ensuring your dodges and attacks feel sharper but no less punishing. Hornet's needle strikes demand the same precision, with no nerfs to enemy patterns or health bars that have players raging online.

Controller Changes Hit Hard

GamingOnLinux highlighted the beta's big shift: a complete rework of controller inputs to fix responsiveness issues. For UK gamers grinding on PS5 or Switch after school, this means smoother silk-casting and pogo jumps, but tougher timing against foes like the silk-winged terrors in Pharloom's depths. No difficulty sliders here—Team Cherry's notes confirm it's all about polishing the core challenge, keeping that Hollow Knight soul-crushing vibe alive since the full release on September 4, 2025.

Global Buzz and UK Grinds

Across the world, players are testing the beta, with Eurogamer noting it's "still a little hard to believe Silksong is here—and tough as nails." In the UK, where rainy afternoons fuel marathon sessions, this patch amps up the replay value without babying you. Posts on X echo the sentiment: one from GameSpot at 8:02 PM GMT yesterday called it out straight—no easier modes, just refined brutality. Impacts? Expect longer boss fights in areas like the weaving districts, where a single mistimed weave can send you back to the last bench, soul empty and frustration high.

What Team Cherry Fixed (and Didn't)

The patch notes detail bug squashes from Patch 3, like stabilising Hornet's thread abilities during intense platforming. But crucially, no reductions in difficulty spikes that make Silksong a Metroidvania masterpiece. Wikipedia confirms the game's scope exploded from DLC to full sequel, and this beta keeps that ambition raw. Quotes from devs via updates stress balance: "We're killing more bugs, not the challenge." For daily grinds, it means adapting to stickier controls that reward practice over luck.

Watch for the full Patch 4 rollout soon—Team Cherry's rapid updates suggest more tweaks, but don't hold your breath for mercy. If you're a 12-year-old UK bug-hunter, grab the beta on Steam or console previews and brace for Pharloom's unyielding trials. This patch proves Silksong isn't going soft; it's sharpening its needle for the long haul.
