Episode three: particle model of matter. Rapid fire.

Density equals mass divided by volume. Unit kilograms per cubic metre. Same material, same density.

Solids: particles close, fixed positions, vibrate. Liquids: close, move past each other. Gases: far apart, random rapid motion.

Heating increases internal energy — the total kinetic and potential energy of all particles. Temperature measures average kinetic energy.

Specific heat capacity: the energy needed to raise one kilogram by one degree Celsius. E equals m c delta theta.

Changing state at constant temperature uses latent heat, not temperature change. Energy breaks or makes bonds between particles.

Gas pressure is caused by particle collisions with walls. Increasing temperature at fixed volume increases pressure. Increasing volume at fixed temperature decreases pressure.

When you compress a gas quickly, work is done on it — temperature can rise. When a gas expands, it does work — temperature can fall.
