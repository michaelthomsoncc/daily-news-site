Physics benchmark, episode one: energy. Rapid fire.

Energy is stored in different ways: kinetic, gravitational, elastic, thermal, chemical, magnetic, electrostatic, and nuclear. Name the store before the transfer happens.

Energy is conserved. It is never created or destroyed — only shifted between stores or dissipated to the surroundings, often as wasted thermal energy.

Efficiency equals useful energy output divided by total energy input. Multiply by a hundred for a percentage. Higher efficiency means less wasted energy.

Power is the rate of energy transfer. Power equals energy divided by time. In electricity, power also equals current times potential difference: P equals I V.

When you heat a substance, the energy needed depends on mass, specific heat capacity, and temperature change: E equals m c delta theta.

Renewable resources replenish quickly: solar, wind, hydro, waves, tides, geothermal, and bio-fuel. Non-renewable resources run out: coal, oil, natural gas, and nuclear fuel rods.

Sankey diagrams show energy flow. The input bar is one hundred percent. Useful output and wasted energy must add up to the full input.
