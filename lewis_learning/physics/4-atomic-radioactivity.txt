Episode four: atomic structure and radioactivity. Rapid fire.

Atoms: proton number defines the element; nucleon number is protons plus neutrons. Isotopes: same protons, different neutrons.

Rutherford scattering showed a tiny, dense, positively charged nucleus. Most of the atom is empty space.

Alpha radiation: helium nucleus, two protons two neutrons. Charge plus two. Heaviest, most ionising, least penetrating — stopped by paper or skin.

Beta minus: neutron becomes proton and electron. Medium ionisation. Stopped by thin aluminium.

Gamma: electromagnetic wave. Least ionising, most penetrating — reduced by thick lead or concrete.

Radioactive decay is random for individual nuclei. Half-life: time for half the nuclei in a sample to decay, or half the activity.

Nuclear equations must balance nucleon number and proton number on both sides.

Fission: large nucleus splits, releases energy, can trigger chain reactions. Fusion: small nuclei join — happens in stars, hard to achieve on Earth.

Uses: medicine, sterilisation, smoke alarms, power. Hazards: ionisation damage to living tissue.
