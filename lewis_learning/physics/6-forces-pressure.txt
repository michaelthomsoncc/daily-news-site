Episode six: weight, pressure, and moments. Rapid fire.

Mass is kilograms — amount of matter. Weight is the force of gravity. W equals m g. Weight is newtons. On Earth, g is about nine point eight metres per second squared.

A free-body diagram shows all forces on one object as arrows from a point.

Pressure equals force divided by area: p equals F over A. Unit pascal. Same force, smaller area, greater pressure.

Moments turn objects. Moment equals force times perpendicular distance from pivot. Unit newton-metre.

Principle of moments: clockwise moments equal anticlockwise moments when balanced.

Hooke's law: force proportional to extension up to the limit of proportionality. F equals k e. Beyond elastic limit, spring may not return.

Centre of mass: point where entire weight appears to act. Stable objects: low centre of mass, wide base.

Hydraulics: pressure transmitted through liquid. Same pressure throughout — large force possible with bigger piston area.
