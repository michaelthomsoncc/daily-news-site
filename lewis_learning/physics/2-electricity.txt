Episode two: electricity. Rapid fire.

Electric current is the rate of flow of charge. Charge is measured in coulombs. Current is measured in amperes. Convention: current flows from positive to negative.

Potential difference, or voltage, is the energy transferred per coulomb of charge. Unit: the volt. V equals E divided by Q.

Resistance opposes current. Ohm's law: V equals I R. Resistance is measured in ohms.

In a series circuit: current is the same everywhere; potential differences add up; resistance adds up. One break stops the whole circuit.

In a parallel circuit: potential difference is the same across each branch; current splits; total current is the sum of branch currents. Resistance of parallel branches gives a smaller total resistance.

Electrical power: P equals I V. Also P equals I squared R, and P equals V squared over R.

UK mains supply is about two hundred thirty volts alternating current. Frequency fifty hertz. Live wire brown, neutral blue, earth green and yellow.

Static electricity: like charges repel, unlike attract. Electrons transfer to create charge imbalance.
