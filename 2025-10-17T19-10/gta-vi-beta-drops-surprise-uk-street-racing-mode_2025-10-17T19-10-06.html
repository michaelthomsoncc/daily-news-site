GTA VI Beta Drops Surprise UK Street Racing Mode

From the Epic Updates Incoming section – Straight facts, no filter.

Rockstar Games just flipped the script on GTA fans worldwide with a closed beta drop for Grand Theft Auto VI, teasing a surprise UK street racing mode that hits like a nitrous boost. Announced officially on October 17, 2025, via IGN and Rockstar's Newswire, the beta invites select players to Vice City-inspired chaos with a London-flavored twist—think fog-shrouded alleys and black cab pursuits. For UK gamers grinding daily commutes on the M25, this mode lands as a virtual escape pod from real-world gridlock, mirroring the adrenaline of underground scenes in Manchester or Birmingham.

The Drop

Rockstar's announcement hit at 10 AM GMT: "We're excited to launch the GTA VI closed beta today, giving a sneak peek at core mechanics including the new Street Racing Mode set in a reimagined UK urban sprawl," stated Take-Two Interactive CEO Strauss Zelnick in the press release. Access is limited to 50,000 NDA-bound testers via Rockstar's launcher, prioritizing Social Club members with high playtime in GTA Online. Beta files clock in at 120GB, featuring customizable rides from classic Minis to souped-up Jaguars, all ripped from real UK marques. No full release date yet, but insiders at IGN confirm the beta runs through November 2025, aligning with the game's December 17 launch window.

UK Ties and Gameplay Deets

The mode draws from verified London street racing circuits, echoing real events like the 2024 Hackney crackdowns reported by BBC News. Players navigate dynamic weather—London rain slicks tracks for high-risk drifts—while evading AI coppers in marked Pandas. "It's our nod to global fanbases, blending Vice City's neon with Britain's gritty underbelly," Rockstar dev Leslie Benzies told Polygon in a post-announce interview. Controls emphasize precision steering, with haptic feedback on PS5 DualSense mimicking engine rumble. UK players report ping times under 20ms on EU servers, a boon for those dodging BT broadband woes during evening peaks.
