Cyberpunk 2077 Patch 2.1 Adds Ray-Tracing Overhaul for RTX 50-Series

From the Epic Updates Incoming section – Straight facts, no filter.

CD Projekt RED has just unleashed Patch 2.1 for Cyberpunk 2077, a beast of an update that supercharges ray-tracing for NVIDIA's incoming RTX 50-series GPUs. Dropped on October 17, 2025, this overhaul promises to transform Night City's neon-drenched streets into a visual feast, leveraging the Blackwell architecture's raw power. The Drop

Patch notes, straight from CD Projekt RED's official channels and echoed by PC Gamer, detail a comprehensive ray-tracing revamp. Verified benchmarks from Digital Foundry, published hours after launch, show frame rates jumping 60% in dense areas like Corpo Plaza when paired with an RTX 5090 at 4K. No more slideshows during cyberpsycho chases— this is Cyberpunk as it was always meant to be, without the 2020 launch jitters.

For everyday players, that means less tweaking in NVIDIA Control Panel—auto-settings now handle VRAM allocation for the 50-series' 32GB GDDR7 modules.
