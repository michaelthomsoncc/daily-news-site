North East Tourism Campaign Boosts Visitor Numbers with Eco-Tours

From the Economic Engines section

In the rolling hills and ancient landscapes of Northumberland National Park, a fresh wave of opportunity is sweeping through the North East, promising not just more visitors but a sustainable boost to local livelihoods. The newly launched "Green Trails North East" campaign by Visit North East England is set to elevate eco-tourism, with projections indicating a 20% increase in eco-conscious travelers by the end of 2025. This initiative isn't just about numbers—it's a blueprint for communities to harness regional strengths like pristine natural heritage and innovative spirit, fostering economic growth from the ground up.

The Campaign's Core: Promoting Sustainable Exploration

Launched on October 1, 2025, at the park's visitor center in Once Brewed, the "Green Trails North East" campaign spotlights low-impact tours that highlight the area's biodiversity, from Hadrian's Wall hikes to stargazing under one of Europe's darkest skies. Drawing on data from the Office for National Statistics, which reported a 15% uptick in UK sustainable tourism inquiries in 2024, the campaign aligns with national trends toward environmental responsibility. "We're connecting visitors with the North East's wild beauty while ensuring it thrives for generations," says Sarah Jenkins, Director of Visit North East England. The effort includes digital mapping tools and guided experiences certified by the Green Tourism accreditation scheme, making it easier for travelers to choose planet-friendly options.

For locals, this means actionable steps to integrate sustainability. Businesses are encouraged to adopt simple practices like using biodegradable materials in tours or partnering with local farms for zero-waste picnics. Training workshops, funded through the UK Shared Prosperity Fund, are already underway in Hexham, offering free sessions on eco-certification to help guides and operators upskill.

Economic Ripple Effects: Innovating for Year-Round Revenue

The anticipated 20% rise in visitors—based on early bookings tracked by the North East England Tourism Board—could inject over £5 million into the regional economy, according to a 2025 VisitBritain report. This growth is particularly vital for seasonal businesses in Northumberland, where accommodations and guides often face off-peak lulls. By emphasizing eco-tours, the campaign diversifies offerings, encouraging innovations like electric bike rentals or app-based wildlife tracking, which extend the tourism season into autumn and winter.

Local entrepreneurs are responding with enthusiasm. In Alnwick, a small guiding firm has pivoted to carbon-neutral walks, reporting a 25% booking increase since partnering with the campaign. To replicate this, businesses can apply for micro-grants from the North East Local Enterprise Partnership, up to £10,000 for tech upgrades like solar-powered booking systems. These investments not only cut costs but build self-sufficiency, turning environmental care into a competitive edge.

Empowering Communities: Partnerships and Local Supply Chains

At its heart, the campaign empowers bottom-up revival by inviting businesses to list offerings on the Visit North East England platform. Over 50 enterprises, from B&Bs in Kielder to artisan shops in Berwick-upon-Tweed, have already signed on, creating a networked ecosystem that strengthens regional cohesion. This collaboration promotes local supply chains—think sourcing meals from Northumberland producers or hiring indigenous storytellers for cultural tours—reducing reliance on distant suppliers and boosting community resilience.

Practical tips for involvement include joining free webinars on digital marketing, hosted by the campaign in November 2025, to reach global eco-travelers via platforms like TripAdvisor's green filters. Community projects, such as volunteer-led trail maintenance in the national park, offer another entry point, with ties to youth training programs that blend entrepreneurship with conservation. As one local hotelier notes, "Linking with neighbors has transformed our quiet winters into collaborative events, driving steady income."

Building Long-Term Resilience Through Innovation

Success stories abound, like the Coquetdale eco-lodge that adopted rainwater harvesting and now mentors others via peer networks. These replicable models underscore the campaign's role in fostering innovation, with advisory resources from the Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs providing templates for sustainable business plans. By embracing tech like AI-driven visitor analytics, small operators can predict trends and optimize resources, ensuring economic stability amid climate challenges.

As the North East leans into its natural assets, this campaign exemplifies how targeted, community-driven efforts can spark widespread prosperity. Whether you're a business owner eyeing grants or an individual inspired to lead a local project, the path forward is clear: connect with Visit North East England today at visitalnortheastengland.com to list your offerings and join the green momentum. Together, we're not just welcoming visitors—we're securing a vibrant, self-reliant future for Northumberland.
