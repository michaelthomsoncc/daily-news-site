The Drop

Leaked slides from the event, first reported by Tom's Hardware, confirm the RTX 5090's core specs: 24,576 CUDA cores, a 50% jump from the RTX 4090's 16,384. Clock speeds hit 2.8GHz boost, paired with 32GB of GDDR7 VRAM at 28Gbps. NVIDIA's Jensen Huang teased in a closed demo, "The 50-series redefines ray tracing—50% more cores mean 2x rasterization uplift in real-world tests." Benchmarks leaked show 180fps in 4K Cyberpunk with full RT and DLSS 4, versus 120fps on the 4090. Power draw? A hefty 600W TDP, demanding beefier PSUs for UK builders sourcing from Scan or CCL.

Benchmark Breakdown

Tom's Hardware's exclusive pull from the event details rasterization gains: 50% faster in non-RT workloads like Forza Horizon 5 at 1440p, clocking 320fps. Ray tracing leaps 70% in Control's brutal scenes, thanks to fourth-gen RT cores. "We've seen 5090 prototypes crush 4090 in AI upscaling," noted a source close to NVIDIA's GTC prep. For UK gamers, this translates to smoother 8K streaming on Virgin Media setups or esports edges in Valorant leagues. No official pricing yet, but leaks peg MSRP at £1,699— a 20% hike from the 4090's launch, factoring Brexit-era import duties.
