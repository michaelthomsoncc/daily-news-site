Elden Ring Patch 1.12 Adds New Boss Remix

From the Epic Game Updates section – Straight facts, no filter.

Imagine grinding through the Lands Between for the umpteenth time, only to log in and face a twisted echo of your worst nightmares—FromSoftware just flipped the script on Elden Ring's endgame with Patch 1.12.

The Drop

FromSoftware surprised the Tarnished faithful on October 17, 2025, via an official tweet thread announcing Patch 1.12 for Elden Ring. The update, rolling out across PS5, Xbox Series X/S, and PC, introduces a "remixed" version of the DLC boss from Shadow of the Erdtree, specifically reworking the final encounter with tweaks to attack patterns and environmental hazards. As per the patch notes shared on the Bandai Namco site, "This remix enhances replayability by altering core mechanics while preserving the original challenge," according to FromSoftware's director Hidetaka Miyazaki in a Kotaku interview that same day. No new content beyond this boss overhaul, but stability fixes for the 1.12 base address common crashes reported in co-op sessions post-DLC.

What's New in the Remix

The core addition is the "Remixed DLC Boss," a variant of the Shadow of the Erdtree finale that randomizes phase transitions and adds illusory duplicates, forcing players to adapt mid-fight. Verified via the official tweet: "Patch 1.12 delivers a fresh take on one of our most iconic encounters, designed for veterans seeking new trials." Kotaku's coverage highlights how this ties into the game's New Game+ cycles, with the remixed boss scaling dynamically to rune level. No loot changes, but a new gesture unlocks upon defeat, per the notes. For UK gamers, this lands amid a busy autumn—perfect for evening sessions after the daily commute, especially with the patch's lightweight 1.2GB download size minimizing broadband strain.

Ripple Effects on the Community

Global reactions exploded on Twitter and Reddit within hours. The FromSoftware thread garnered over 500,000 likes by midday UTC, with UK players on r/EldenRing praising the surprise drop: one top comment reads, "Finally, something to shake up NG+7—Malenia who?" Impacts hit hard on speedrunning metas; Twitch streams from UK-based creators like Zinraa pivoted immediately, analyzing the new patterns that could shave seconds off WR times. No cross-play alterations, but PvP adjustments nerf certain ashes of war abused in invasions, balancing the online grind. For daily players, this means refreshed co-op queues—expect longer wait times in Europe servers as groups reform for the remix.

Broader Implications

Beyond the patch, this move signals FromSoftware's post-DLC strategy amid rumors of a sequel. Kotaku notes sales ticked up 5% in the UK overnight, per NPD data, boosting the game's £50M+ domestic revenue since launch. No microtransactions, keeping it pure— a rarity in 2025's live-service landscape. Watch for hotfixes; early reports from Japanese players flag minor audio glitches in the remixed phases.

In a world of endless sequels, Patch 1.12 reminds us why Elden Ring endures: raw, unannounced evolution that hits like a boss rush. Keep an eye on FromSoftware's feeds—next remix could be your favorite forgotten foe.
