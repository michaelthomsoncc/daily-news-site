Regional Mayors Unite for Cross-Border Trade Initiative

Streamlining local business collaborations from Northumberland to Durham to boost supply chains and economic growth.

In a bold step toward fostering regional prosperity, mayors across the Northumbria area—from Northumberland to Durham—have announced a groundbreaking cross-border trade pact. Launched on October 15, 2025, at a joint summit in Newcastle, this initiative promises to simplify business interactions, reduce logistical hurdles, and unlock new opportunities for local enterprises. As supply chain disruptions from global events continue to challenge the economy, this pact arrives as a timely beacon, empowering communities to build resilient, self-sufficient networks right on their doorsteps. For businesses and entrepreneurs, it's not just policy—it's a practical pathway to innovation and growth.

A Unified Vision for Streamlined Trade

The pact, formally titled the North East Cross-Border Commerce Alliance, involves mayors from key councils including Northumberland, North Tyneside, Newcastle upon Tyne, Gateshead, Sunderland, and County Durham. Drawing on the region's strengths in manufacturing, agriculture, and renewable energy, the agreement aims to harmonize regulations for easier goods movement and shared procurement processes. According to a report from the North East Local Enterprise Partnership (LEP) released last week, this could shave up to 20% off intra-regional transport costs, based on pilot data from similar initiatives in the Midlands.

"This isn't about red tape—it's about removing barriers so our local firms can thrive together," said Northumberland County Council Leader, Glen Sanderson, during the launch. The initiative builds on the success of the North East Growth Hub, which has already supported over 5,000 businesses since 2020 with digital tools and funding advice. By integrating trade facilitation services, the pact enhances efficiency, allowing small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) to access broader markets without the complexities of international shipping.

Empowering Businesses with Actionable Opportunities

For local firms looking to capitalize, the pact opens doors to targeted networking events scheduled through the North East Growth Hub website (northeastgrowthhub.org.uk). Starting November 2025, monthly virtual and in-person sessions will connect suppliers from rural Northumberland farms with urban manufacturers in Durham, focusing on sectors like sustainable food production and green tech.

Practical tip: Businesses can begin by registering for the Hub's free trade diagnostic tool, which assesses supply chain gaps and matches partners. Grants are available via the UK Shared Prosperity Fund—up to £10,000 for collaborative projects—prioritizing innovations in local sourcing. One replicable success story comes from Hexham-based artisan cheese producer Tillbrook's, which partnered with a Durham logistics firm last year, cutting delivery times by 30% and boosting revenue through joint marketing. Entrepreneurs are encouraged to explore training programs at local colleges, such as those offered by Northumberland College on digital inventory management, to adopt tech that streamlines operations.

Fostering Innovation and Community Cohesion

Beyond trade, the initiative promotes grassroots economic expansion by emphasizing sustainable practices and community projects. Mayors highlighted how the pact aligns with the region's net-zero goals, incentivizing eco-friendly supply chains—think electric vehicle fleets for cross-border deliveries or zero-waste packaging collaborations. Durham Mayor, Amanda Hopgood, noted, "We're investing in our people, creating jobs that last by blending tradition with forward-thinking innovation."

Advisory how-to: Individuals and startups should join community-led workshops on entrepreneurship, like those at the Growth Hub's Innovation Labs in Sunderland, where participants learn to prototype local supply solutions. For self-sufficiency, consider forming co-operatives: a model proven in Tees Valley, where farmer groups now supply 40% of regional grocers directly, reducing reliance on distant imports. These steps not only drive economic resilience but also strengthen social ties, turning neighbors into partners in progress.

Building Long-Term Regional Resilience

As the Northumbria region navigates post-pandemic recovery, this trade pact stands as a testament to collaborative spirit. With implications for thousands of jobs—projected growth of 15% in SME output by 2027, per LEP forecasts—it underscores the power of bottom-up revival. By leveraging natural assets like the area's fertile lands and skilled workforce, communities are positioning themselves for sustained success.

Now is the time to act: Visit the North East Growth Hub site today to sign up for events, apply for grants, or connect with potential partners. Together, we're not just trading goods—we're trading in a brighter, more connected future for Northumbria.
