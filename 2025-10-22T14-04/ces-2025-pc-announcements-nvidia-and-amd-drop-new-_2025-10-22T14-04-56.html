Imagine firing up your favourite game like Fortnite or Minecraft with graphics so smooth and sharp they feel real. That's the buzz from CES 2025, where NVIDIA and AMD just unveiled their latest PC powerhouses. NVIDIA's RTX 5000 Series Takes Centre Stage

NVIDIA revealed its RTX 5000-series graphics cards at the expo, packing advanced AI tech for ray tracing that makes lights and shadows pop in games. These cards promise faster rendering, meaning less lag in fast-paced titles like Call of Duty. For UK gamers, this means upgrading your PC could handle 4K gaming without breaking a sweat, perfect for streaming on Twitch from your bedroom setup. The series builds on previous leaks, focusing on efficiency to keep power bills in check amid rising energy costs.

AMD Counters with Ryzen and Radeon Upgrades

AMD followed suit with its own new Ryzen GPUs and Radeon GPUs, integrating better CPU-GPU teamwork for seamless multitasking. Ryzen updates feature more cores for quicker load times in open-world games like Roblox adventures. Radeon cards shine in colour accuracy, ideal for creative mods in Minecraft. In the UK, where PC building is huge in gaming communities, these drops could lower entry barriers with competitive pricing against NVIDIA, letting more kids join high-end play without huge spends.
