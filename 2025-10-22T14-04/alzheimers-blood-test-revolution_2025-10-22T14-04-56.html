The Big Approval That Kicked It Off

Back in May 2025, the FDA greenlit the world's first blood test for Alzheimer's. No more waiting for pricey brain scans or spinal taps—this simple blood draw spots telltale proteins that signal the disease. It's a game-changer for early diagnosis, especially for folks over 55 showing signs like forgetfulness or confusion. Doctors say if insurance kicks in soon, it'll roll out fast as the go-to check-up tool. In the UK, where dementia affects over 900,000 people, this could mean quicker NHS referrals and less strain on daily lives, like helping grandparents stay sharp for family game nights.
