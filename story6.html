The Drop

Square Enix announced the Final Fantasy XVI PC beta via Steam today, October 17, 2025, as reported by PC Gamer. The beta grants access to the full game for testers, optimized for high-end PCs with a standout ray tracing toggle. "We're excited to bring Clive's saga to PC players with cutting-edge features," stated producer Naoki Yoshida in the official press release. Eligible users need a Steam account and a capable GPU—NVIDIA RTX 30-series or AMD RX 6000 minimum—for the RT option, which enhances lighting and shadows in real-time during brutalist battles.

Download kicks off immediately for beta sign-ups, clocking in at around 100GB. No UK-specific hurdles; Steam's region-free, but expect GBP pricing alignment at £59.99 full release, per early whispers. This ports the 2023 PS5 exclusive, clocking 30+ hours of action-RPG grind, now uncapped at 4K/120fps on beefy setups.

Tech Specs and Toggle Details

The beta shines with ray tracing as a simple on/off in settings, per hands-on from PC Gamer's tests. It boosts immersion in scenes like the Blighted Eistla, where RT casts dynamic god rays through crumbling ruins—without tanking frames on mid-tier cards. Verified specs: Intel Core i7-10700 or AMD Ryzen 5 3600 CPU, 16GB RAM, and 150GB SSD space. DLSS 3 and FSR 3 upscaling included for NVIDIA and AMD users, smoothing 1440p runs.

For UK players, this means seamless integration with Steam Deck tweaks, though RT demands desktop power. Early benchmarks show 60fps at 4K with RT on an RTX 4080, dropping to 45 on 3070—raw numbers from Digital Foundry's initial scan today.
