Razer Unveils Phantom White Translucent Gaming Peripherals

From the Hardware Hits 2025 section – Straight facts, no filter.

Razer has dropped the Phantom White collection, a fresh translucent lineup hitting the gaming scene just yesterday. For UK gamers eyeing global gear upgrades, this means see-through peripherals that blend retro vibes with high-spec performance, all for a modest premium. Launched on October 16, 2025, the collection expands Razer's Phantom series, originally unveiled earlier this year, bringing that milky white translucency to everyday setups without breaking the bank.

Collection Overview

The Phantom White collection reimagines gaming aesthetics with a focus on translucent design, evoking early 2000s nostalgia while fitting modern rigs. Razer describes it as "the best of gaming aesthetics, reimagined," emphasizing a retro-futuristic look that lets internal components shine through. This isn't just cosmetic—it's an evolution of the Phantom line, broadening options for builders who want a cohesive, ethereal white theme across their desks. For UK players grinding through long sessions in titles like Valorant or Elden Ring, this setup promises durability meets style, cutting down on the visual clutter of opaque blacks and grays that dominate most peripherals.

Standout Peripherals

Leading the pack is the Basilisk V3 Pro 35K mouse, now in Phantom White. This wireless beast packs Razer's latest 35,000 DPI sensor for pinpoint accuracy, ideal for competitive edges in fast-paced shooters. Paired with it is the BlackWidow V4 75% keyboard, a compact mechanical board with translucent keys that reveal the RGB underglow. Both maintain full Razer Synapse compatibility for customizable lighting and macros, ensuring seamless integration into daily grinds. Other mentions in the lineup hint at broader compatibility, like matching white variants for mice and boards, allowing full translucent builds that pop under UK desk lamps or during late-night streams.

Pricing and Premium Details

Razer prices the Phantom White option at a $10 to $20 uplift over standard colors, keeping entry accessible for budget-conscious gamers. The Basilisk V3 Pro 35K in this finish starts around the premium tier, while the BlackWidow V4 75% follows suit—exact UK conversions hover near £100-£150 depending on retailer markups. Availability kicks off immediately via Razer's site and partners like Amazon, with posts confirming stock for quick shipping. This low premium means no major hit to wallets, letting everyday UK setups evolve without the full cost of new hardware, especially as winter gaming marathons loom.

Global Buzz and UK Relevance

Word spread fast on platforms like X, with Razer's ANZ account pushing "Build your full translucent setup now," racking up views and favorites overnight. Tom's Hardware noted the launch as a direct response to demand for aesthetic variety, while sites like 9to5Toys and Guru3D highlighted the "white see-through peripherals with retro-futuristic vibes." For UK gamers tracking global moves, this lands as a timely refresh—amid rising energy costs for PC runs, translucent whites reflect light better, subtly aiding visibility in dim rooms without extra bulbs. It's a nod to personalization in a market flooded with cookie-cutter blacks.

Watch for stock rushes and potential bundle deals; this collection cements Razer's grip on style-forward gaming, proving small tweaks like translucency can refresh daily play without overhauling your rig.
