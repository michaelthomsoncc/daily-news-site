Launch Breakdown: Pokémon Legends: Z-A Hits the Ground Running

Pokémon Legends: Z-A, developed by Game Freak and published by Nintendo and The Pokémon Company, arrived exclusively for the original Nintendo Switch and an enhanced port for the Switch 2 on October 16. Set entirely in Lumiose City, it serves as a sequel to Pokémon X and Y from 2013 and the second in the Legends sub-series after Arceus in 2022. Early reviews praise the revamped battle system for its fluid, open-world feel, but note the urban confines limit visual exploration compared to sprawling predecessors. TechRadar's live coverage captures the buzz: "I've been playing Pokémon Legends Z-A since launch, here’s the best Starter to choose, Mystery Gift codes to redeem, and my early impressions." UK pre-orders spiked in the final days, with Nintendo's eShop seeing heavy traffic as players snag the title amid rising energy costs squeezing gaming budgets.
